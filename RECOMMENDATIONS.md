# What You Can Actually Do: Evidence-Based Recommendations

**Last verified: March 2026 | Every recommendation below is backed by human RCT or large prospective cohort data.**

This is not a supplement list. This is not biohacker speculation. These are the interventions with the strongest published evidence for slowing biological aging, reducing all-cause mortality, and extending healthspan — things you can start today, for free or near-free.

Recommendations are ordered by evidence strength. Each includes specific doses, frequencies, and effect sizes from the data.

### 5. Don't Smoke / Quit Smoking

Smoking accelerates every hallmark of aging simultaneously — DNA damage, telomere shortening, epigenetic aging, inflammation, senescence, mitochondrial dysfunction. If you smoke, quitting is the single highest-impact intervention available.

- Current active smokers show significant epigenetic age acceleration across multiple clocks (Perez-Garcia et al. 2025, PMID: 41445615)
- **Intergenerational effect**: Paternal smoking accelerates biological aging *in newborns* — telomere shortening documented in offspring (Stagnar et al. 2026, PMID: 41232904)
- Cessation shows partial recovery in epigenetic age markers — it's never too late to quit
- Secondhand smoke exposure in childhood accelerates epigenetic aging (Patil et al. 2025, PMID: 41281718)

## Tier 2: Strong Observational Evidence + Mechanistic Support

### 7. Social Connection and Purpose

This is not soft advice. The effect sizes rival smoking and obesity.

**Social isolation and mortality** (Wang et al. 2023, 90 cohort studies, 2.2 million participants, PMID: 37337095):
- Social isolation: **HR 1.32** (95% CI 1.26-1.39) for all-cause mortality
- Loneliness: **HR 1.14** (95% CI 1.08-1.20) for all-cause mortality
- Social isolation + breast cancer: **HR 1.51** (95% CI 1.34-1.70)

**Strong social relationships are protective** (Holt-Lunstad et al. 2010, 148 studies, 308,849 participants, PMID: 20668659):
- Complex social integration: **OR 1.91** (95% CI 1.63-2.23) — a 91% increased survival probability
- This effect size **exceeds typical smoking and obesity estimates**

**Purpose in life** (Cohen et al. 2016, meta-analysis, 136,265 participants, PMID: 26630073):
- All-cause mortality: **RR 0.83** (95% CI 0.75-0.91) — 17% reduced mortality risk
- Cardiovascular events: **RR 0.83** (95% CI 0.75-0.92)

**Recommendation**: Invest in relationships. Find or maintain purpose. This is not optional for longevity — isolation kills at rates comparable to a pack-a-day habit.

### 9. Stress Management

Chronic stress accelerates biological aging through inflammation and telomere shortening.

- **Caregiving stress → telomere shortening**: Chronic stress drives NF-κB, IL-1β, IL-6 expression, which predicts telomere attrition over 15 months (Lin et al. 2018, PMID: 29980010)
- **Meditation preserves telomeres**: Loving-kindness meditation RCT (12 weeks, n=142) showed significantly less telomere attrition vs. controls (Le Nguyen et al. 2019, PMID: 31185369). 18-month Age-Well trial (n=137, 65+) showed meditation attendance correlated with telomere preservation (r=0.45, Kaliman et al. 2024, PMID: 39582797).
- **Exercise reverses stress-related telomere shortening** in caregivers (Puterman et al. 2018, PMID: 30266522)
- **Mindfulness reverses stress-induced epigenetic changes** (Househam 2023, PMID: 36863798)

**Recommendation**: Any consistent stress-reduction practice — meditation, yoga, therapy, time in nature. The mechanism (cortisol → inflammation → telomere shortening) is well-established. Exercise doubles as both fitness and stress management.

### 10. Gut Health

Gut microbiome diversity declines with age and correlates with longevity. Centenarians have distinct microbiome profiles across multiple cultures (Italian, Chinese, Japanese, Sardinian cohorts).

- **Mendelian randomization** now supports **causal** (not just correlational) links between specific microbiome features and longevity (Liu et al. 2023, PMID: 36991009)
- **Akkermansia muciniphila**: First human RCT (pasteurized strain, 12 weeks) improved muscle strength in sarcopenia-prone older adults (Kang et al. 2024, PMID: 39683431). Promising but early.
- **Mediterranean diet increases microbiome diversity** and Akkermansia abundance (Lauria et al. 2026, PMID: 41381307)
- **Diversity of fiber sources** matters more than absolute dose — plant-based variety is key (Beke & Dahl 2026, PMID: 40459489)

**Recommendation**: Eat diverse plant fiber (vegetables, legumes, whole grains, nuts, seeds). Include fermented foods (yogurt, kimchi, sauerkraut). The Mediterranean diet pattern consistently improves gut microbiome profiles.

### 11. Oral Health

Periodontal disease drives systemic inflammation through the same pathways (NF-κB, IL-6, TNF-α) that fuel inflammaging.

- Tooth loss and periodontal disease associated with all-cause and CVD mortality in UK/USA cohorts over 9-15 year follow-up (Kotronia et al. 2021, PMID: 34385519)
- Periodontal pathogens enter the bloodstream and trigger endothelial dysfunction (Foroughi & Parang 2026, PMID: 41851576)
- Periodontitis significantly associated with mortality in elderly >75 years (Qi et al. 2020, PMID: 32020486)

**Recommendation**: Brush, floss, get regular dental care. Periodontal disease is a treatable, avoidable source of chronic systemic inflammation.

## Tier 3: Promising but Less Certain

### 12. Heat Exposure (Sauna)

The Finnish sauna data is striking but may be partially confounded by healthy-user bias.

**Kuopio Ischemic Heart Disease Study** (Laukkanen et al. 2015, 2,315 men, 20.7-year follow-up, PMID: 25705824):
- 4-7x/week vs 1x/week: **HR 0.37** (95% CI 0.18-0.75) for sudden cardiac death
- 2-3x/week: **HR 0.71** (95% CI 0.52-0.98) for all-cause mortality
- Duration >19 min/session: **HR 0.48** (95% CI 0.31-0.75) for sudden cardiac death

**Gender-inclusive cohort** (2018, 1,688 participants, 15-year follow-up, PMID: 30486813):
- 4-7x/week: **HR 0.23** (95% CI 0.08-0.65) for CVD mortality after full adjustment

**Causality question**: Associations survived adjustment for physical activity, SES, and CVD risk factors. Sauna frequency independently improved cardiorespiratory fitness (+0.84-1.17 mL/kg/min, Kunutsor 2024, PMID: 38836690). Likely partially causal, partially confounded.

**Recommendation**: If accessible, 2-3+ sessions/week at 80-90°C for 15-20 minutes. Not a substitute for exercise. Finnish-style dry sauna is the studied modality — infrared saunas have less data.

### 13. Cold Exposure

**No human mortality or longevity data exists.** Zero prospective studies on intentional cold exposure and lifespan.

- Cold water immersion studies are limited to acute biomarkers and recovery
- Brown fat activation is established but not linked to human lifespan
- Cold-related excess winter mortality in elderly argues for caution, not enthusiasm
- A 2025 review (PMID: 39884345) examines theoretical mechanisms but acknowledges the absence of human longevity data

**Recommendation**: No evidence-based recommendation possible. Cold exposure is not harmful in moderation for healthy individuals, but claims about longevity benefits are unsupported by human data.

### 14. Sun Exposure / Vitamin D

A surprising finding: **vitamin D supplements don't help, but sun avoidance may kill**.

**Vitamin D supplementation — RCTs are negative:**
- VITAL trial (largest RCT): No mortality benefit from 2000 IU/day vitamin D3
- D-Health trial (21,315 older adults, PMID: 35026158): No reduction in all-cause mortality
- 2026 meta-analysis (PMID: 41719624): When confounding is removed through randomization, vitamin D benefits disappear
- Relationship is non-linear — increased risk at LOW levels, limited benefit beyond sufficiency (Dalamaga et al. 2026, PMID: 41849024)

**Sun avoidance — as dangerous as smoking:**
- Swedish cohort (Lindqvist et al. 2016, PMID: 26992108): **Women who avoided sun had life expectancy similar to smokers.** 0.6-2.1 years reduced life expectancy.
- **UK Biobank replication** (Stevenson et al. 2024, PMID: 39094281): UV exposure inversely associated with all-cause, CVD, and cancer mortality. Confirms Swedish finding in different population.

**Recommendation**: Get regular moderate sun exposure. Don't supplement vitamin D unless deficient (get tested). Don't avoid the sun entirely — the mortality data suggests sun avoidance is a greater risk than sun exposure for most people. This does not mean ignore skin cancer risk — it means balance.

## What to Avoid: Biggest Aging Accelerators

Based on epigenetic clock and biomarker data, these are the largest avoidable accelerators of biological aging:

| Accelerator | Effect | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Smoking** | Strongest and most consistent epigenetic age acceleration across all clocks. Intergenerational effects on offspring. | PMID: 41445615, 41232904 |
| **Social isolation** | HR 1.32 for all-cause mortality (2.2M participants). Effect size rivals smoking. | PMID: 37337095 |
| **Air pollution (PM2.5)** | +0.74 years epigenetic age per 5 μg/m³ in adults (meta-analysis trend, not yet statistically significant). | PMID: 41564677 |
| **Pesticide exposure** | +1.72 years epigenetic age acceleration from herbicides (Lifelines cohort). Occupational hazard. | PMID: 41271129 |
| **Heavy metal exposure** | Chronic Pb/Hg/Cd mixture triggers premature brain aging and telomere shortening. | PMID: 40393628, 39068742 |
| **Chronic stress** | Drives NF-κB/IL-6 inflammation → telomere shortening. Caregivers show accelerated cellular aging. | PMID: 29980010 |
| **Periodontal disease** | Chronic source of systemic IL-6/TNF-α. Associated with CVD and all-cause mortality. | PMID: 34385519 |

## What NOT to Bother With (Evidence Doesn't Support)

Based on our verification work, the following popular interventions lack evidence of functional benefit in humans:

| Intervention | Why Not |
|---|---|
| NMN/NR supplements | Two meta-analyses: zero functional benefits. NAD+ goes up; nothing measurable improves. |
| Resveratrol | Original SIRT1 activation was an assay artifact. GSK program failed. ~1% bioavailability. |
| Fisetin supplements | Unreplicated mouse study. Only human study (n=10) was negative. Not ITP-tested. |
| Metformin (if you exercise) | Blunts exercise adaptations (VO2 max, mitochondrial respiration). May do net harm. |
| GDF11 anything | Debunked. Exacerbates cardiac injury. |
| Growth hormone for anti-aging | TRIIM was n=9 no controls. GH/IGF-1 paradox: reduced GH extends lifespan in every model organism. Increases cancer risk. |
| Unregulated stem cell treatments | 35 documented adverse events including blindness and death. No efficacy proven. |
